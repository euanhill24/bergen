## Practical Info

### Weather (Mid-April)
- **Temperature**: 3-10°C (37-50°F). Can feel colder with wind on mountains and fjords
- **Rain**: Bergen averages ~170 rain days per year. April is one of the drier months but still expect rain on roughly half the days
- **Daylight**: ~14-15 hours of daylight (sunrise ~06:15, sunset ~20:45). Long, bright days
- **Snow**: Unlikely at sea level, possible on mountain tops

### What to Pack
- Waterproof jacket (the single most important item)
- Layers: base layer, fleece/wool mid-layer, waterproof outer
- Waterproof shoes or boots with good grip
- Compact umbrella
- Warm hat and gloves for mountain tops and fjord cruises
- Something smart-casual for the birthday dinner

### Getting Around
- **Walking**: Bergen's centre is very walkable. Most attractions are 10-20 minutes on foot from the house
- **Airport transfer**: Bybanen light rail runs directly from Bergen Airport (Flesland) to city centre, ~45 min, ~42 NOK. Very easy
- **Public transport**: Skyss runs buses and the light rail. Single ticket ~42 NOK. Day passes available
- **Bergen Card**: Consider for 24/48/72 hours — includes free/discounted museum entry and free public transport. Can pay for itself in 1-2 days
- **Taxis**: Expensive (as with all of Norway). Use for convenience, not economy

### Budget Heads-Up
Norway is expensive. Some rough guides:
- Coffee: 50-65 NOK (~£4-5)
- Casual lunch: 150-250 NOK (~£12-20)
- Dinner main course: 250-400 NOK (~£20-32)
- Fine dining tasting menu: 1,200-1,500 NOK (~£95-120)
- Beer at a bar: 90-120 NOK (~£7-10)
- Wine by the glass: 100-150 NOK (~£8-12)
- Tip: Groceries from Rema 1000, Kiwi, or Meny are much cheaper — stock up for breakfasts and snacks

## Lunch Spots

### Pingvinen
- **What it is**: Traditional Norwegian "husmanskost" (home cooking)
- **Why go**: This is THE place for authentic Norwegian comfort food. Meatballs, fish soup, cod, lamb stew. Cosy, unpretentious, beloved by locals. Can get busy so arrive early or book
- **Price**: Mains ~180-250 NOK
- **Area**: Central Bergen

### Fisketorget (Fish Market)
- **What it is**: Bergen's famous indoor/outdoor fish market
- **Why go**: A Bergen institution. Sample fresh seafood, fish & chips, the legendary fish soup, king crab legs. A bit touristy but it's a must-do at least once. Great for a casual, walk-around lunch
- **Price**: ~150-300 NOK depending on what you try
- **Area**: Harbour front

### Bryggeloftet & Stuene
- **What it is**: Traditional Norwegian restaurant in a historic Bryggen building
- **Why go**: Beautiful old building, proper sit-down lunch in a historic setting. Fish soup, open-faced sandwiches (smørbrød), traditional dishes. Good for a more substantial lunch
- **Price**: ~200-350 NOK
- **Area**: Bryggen

### Zupperia
- **What it is**: Soup-focused lunch spot
- **Why go**: Hearty, warming soups perfect for Bergen's unpredictable weather. Good value by Bergen standards. Quick and satisfying
- **Price**: ~130-180 NOK
- **Area**: City centre

### Trekroneren
- **What it is**: Legendary hot dog kiosk
- **Why go**: A Bergen institution since forever. Not just any hot dogs — proper Norwegian pølse with all the toppings. Cheap, quick, fun. Great for a snack between activities
- **Price**: ~80-120 NOK
- **Area**: Near the fish market

### Lysverket (Lunch Service)
- **What it is**: Modern Nordic cuisine at KODE art museum, casual lunch format
- **Why go**: Get a taste of one of Bergen's best kitchens without the full fine dining price tag. Beautiful setting inside the museum. Great to combine with a KODE visit
- **Price**: ~200-300 NOK for lunch
- **Area**: KODE 4 museum, Lille Lungegårdsvannet

### Bien Snackbar
- **What it is**: Casual, no-fuss sandwich and snack spot
- **Why go**: Good sandwiches, lighter fare, reasonable prices. Handy for a quick refuel without a full sit-down meal
- **Price**: ~100-170 NOK
- **Area**: City centre

- **Why it's special**: One of Bergen's most acclaimed restaurants. Chef Christopher Haatuft creates inventive Nordic dishes with stunning presentation. The setting inside the art museum is beautiful. Tasting menus take you on a journey through Norwegian ingredients. Perfect celebration restaurant

- **What it is**: Upscale seafood and Nordic restaurant

- **Price**: Tasting menu or à la carte, ~800-1,200 NOK per person with drinks

### 1877
- **What it is**: Fine dining in a historic building
- **Why it's special**: Named for the year the building was constructed. Elegant, sophisticated setting. Multi-course tasting menus with wine pairings. Classic fine dining done with Norwegian flair

- **Price**: ~1,500+ NOK per person including boat
- **Atmosphere**: Adventurous, spectacular, once-in-a-lifetime
- **Note**: Seasonal — verify they're open in April and book early

## Activities & Sightseeing

### Must-See

#### Bryggen
- **What**: UNESCO World Heritage wharf — colourful wooden buildings dating back to the Hanseatic trading days
- **Why go**: The icon of Bergen. Wander the narrow alleyways, browse the small shops and workshops. Free to walk around, the Hanseatic Museum costs ~100-120 NOK
- **Time needed**: 1-2 hours
- **Area**: Right next to your accommodation

#### Fløibanen (Fløyen Funicular)
- **What**: Funicular railway to the top of Mount Fløyen (320m)
- **Why go**: Incredible panoramic views of Bergen, the harbour, and surrounding mountains. Walking trails at the top, a cafe, and a troll sculpture. The base station is very close to your house
- **Time needed**: 1-3 hours (quick ride + views, or add a hike)
- **Price**: ~155 NOK return
- **Tip**: Go on a clear day if possible for the best views

#### Bergenhus Fortress
- **What**: Medieval fortress with Håkon's Hall and Rosenkrantz Tower
- **Why go**: Right by your accommodation. Beautiful grounds to walk around (free). The Hall and Tower are worth going inside for the history and views. One of the oldest royal residences in Norway
- **Time needed**: 1-1.5 hours
- **Price**: ~100 NOK each for Hall and Tower, grounds are free

### Nature & Fjords

#### Mostraumen Fjord Cruise
- **What**: 3-hour cruise from Bergen harbour through narrow straits and fjord scenery
- **Why go**: Experience the fjords without a full day trip. Dramatic scenery, waterfalls, narrow passages. Runs year-round. Operated by Rødne
- **Time needed**: 3 hours
- **Price**: ~600-700 NOK
- **Tip**: Book in advance, dress warmly, bring a camera

#### Mount Ulriken Cable Car
- **What**: Cable car to the highest of Bergen's seven mountains (643m)
- **Why go**: Even more dramatic views than Fløyen. On a clear day you can see the ocean and mountain ranges. Cafe at the top. You can hike between Ulriken and Fløyen (the "Vidden" trail, ~4-5 hours) but check for snow in April
- **Time needed**: 1-3 hours
- **Price**: ~200 NOK return

#### Norway in a Nutshell
- **What**: Classic full-day roundtrip combining train, fjord cruise (Nærøyfjord or Aurlandsfjord), and bus
- **Why go**: The ultimate Norwegian fjord experience. Stunning train journey, dramatic fjord cruise, mountain scenery. A full-day commitment but absolutely worth it
- **Time needed**: Full day (8-10 hours)
- **Price**: ~1,800-2,200 NOK
- **Note**: Book well in advance. Check April schedule

#### Stoltzekleiven
- **What**: 900-step stairway up the mountainside
- **Why go**: Free, popular with locals, great workout with rewarding views at the top. Not for the faint-hearted but very satisfying
- **Time needed**: 45 min - 1.5 hours
- **Price**: Free

### Culture & Museums

#### KODE Art Museums
- **What**: Four museum buildings with Norwegian and international art, including Edvard Munch and Nikolai Astrup
- **Why go**: World-class art collection. KODE 4 is the standout building. Beautiful location along the lake. Lysverket restaurant is inside KODE 4
- **Time needed**: 2-3 hours
- **Price**: ~150 NOK

#### Troldhaugen — Edvard Grieg Museum
- **What**: The lakeside home of Norway's most famous composer
- **Why go**: Beautiful setting, fascinating museum, and they sometimes hold piano recitals in the concert hall. The grounds are lovely to walk around. ~20 min from centre by light rail
- **Time needed**: 1.5-2 hours
- **Price**: ~150 NOK
- **Getting there**: Bybanen light rail

#### Theta Museum
- **What**: Tiny museum about the Norwegian WWII resistance movement
- **Why go**: Hidden in a Bryggen alleyway, it tells the story of the Theta resistance group. Small but powerful. Takes about 20-30 minutes
- **Time needed**: 30 min
- **Price**: ~50 NOK

#### Bergen Aquarium (Akvariet)
- **What**: Aquarium with Nordic marine life, penguins, and sea lions
- **Why go**: Fun for a rainy day. Penguins are the highlight. Good if the group wants something lighter
- **Time needed**: 1.5-2 hours
- **Price**: ~310 NOK

### Fun & Social

#### 7 Fjell Bryggeri
- **What**: Bergen's notable craft brewery
- **Why go**: Tours and tastings of locally brewed craft beer. Good for a group activity. Learn about Norwegian brewing culture while sampling the goods
- **Time needed**: 1.5-2 hours
- **Price**: Tours ~200-300 NOK including tastings

#### Food Walking Tour
- **What**: Guided walking tour through Bergen sampling local food
- **Why go**: Great way to discover hidden food spots and learn about Norwegian food culture. Several operators run these (Bergen Food Tours is one)
- **Time needed**: 2-3 hours
- **Price**: ~600-900 NOK

#### Escape Rooms
- **What**: Several escape room venues in central Bergen
- **Why go**: Fun group activity for 7 people, especially on a rainy afternoon. Good for team bonding
- **Time needed**: 1-1.5 hours
- **Price**: ~250-350 NOK per person

#### Skostredet — Creative Quarter
- **What**: Bergen's street art and independent creative district
- **Why go**: Free to wander. Street art, vintage shops, independent boutiques, galleries. Good for an afternoon amble. Kippers coffee shop is here
- **Time needed**: 1-2 hours
- **Price**: Free (unless you shop)
